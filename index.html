About

The SA Ambulance Service is a medical emergency service that cares for South Australians who are injured, suffering or in need of medical assistance by providing access to highly trained professionals.

Timeline

5 week, UX stages were done with a partner. UI conducted individually.

My Role

End-to-end development. From UX to UI design. .

Design Tools

Adobe XD, Miro

BACKGROUND

The goal of this project is to update the SA Ambulance website to create both a far better user experience but also to be more reflective of the company as it stands in 2020.

While there is evidence that the website has been and continues to be updated with new content the website as a whole seems to be approximately 10 years old.

    • 02- Research.

    USABILITY TESTING

    We conducted 6 usability tests focusing on key processes we believed to be the most important to the greatest number of people. The tests highlighted several issues to be addressed in the redesign.

    Most concerning was how the usability difficulties of the website seemed to influence the level of trust users felt towards SA Ambulance as a whole.

    USABILITY QUOTES

    "I have no confidence in this website"

    "I don't trust what they're saying is correct"

    "I'm losing trust in them the more I look at it"

    WEBSITE HEURISTIC EVALUATION

    A heuristic evaluation was conducted analysing and scoring the website against a number of factors that fall under the following broad headings: appearance, content, navigation, functionality and Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics.

    Key areas that did not satisfy the criteria predominantly fell under the aesthetic and information layout category.

    Each category was given a score 1 - 3:

    Does not satisfy the criteria

    47%

    Partially satisfies the criteria

    39%

    satisfies the criteria

    14%

    COLOUR ANALYSIS

    When analysing the website there seemed to be a lot of inconsistency in the specific green being used. Further research was done by reviewing both publications on the Facebook page and more recently published brochures and documents within the website. This inconsistency made it difficult to determine the brand colours.

    03- Ideation.

    NAVIGATION RE-STRUCTURE

    A key change in the restructure of the navigation is the redesign of the News section.

    The news section will be broken down into:

    News

    Our Stories

    Publications

    Reports

    This will be a place where everything from new campaigns and programs to COVID updates can be launched and announced.

    NEW SITE MAP

    The new site map illustrates the overall reduction of pages. Many of these pages are able to be reduced by utilising the main secondary navigation pages (e.g about) to house core information that was previously broken down into individual pages.

    The footer will be utilised to house both the social media links and the link to the staff portal.

    Colour

    SA Ambulance Service utilises green as their core colour as it matches the South Australian paramedic work uniform. For South Australians the colour green is often the most clear association with the Ambulance Service, therefore it is important it is used consistently and correctly throughout publications.

    Columns

    The website redesign utilises a strong column system, both in vertical layout and for horizontal spacing of content

    Desktop

    • Columns: 12
    • Gutter width: 0
    • Column width: 108
    • Margins: 312

    Mobile

    • Columns: 4
    • Gutter width: 0
    • Column width: 84
    • Margins: 19

    06- Conclusion.

    NEXT STEPS

    Scaling up. The next step for this project would be expanding the consistent styling and layout to mock up the remaining pages.

    KEY TAKEAWAYS

    Navigation. One of the core issues with the SA Ambulance website navigation as it currently stands is that there is no central location to post updates, announcements and news. I have realised that building these systems into a website from the beginning they can quickly become cluttered and hard to manage.
